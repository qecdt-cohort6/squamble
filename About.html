About

Full details can be found in the project report.

Squamble is based on graphs that represent quantum circuits.

The basis states of a quantum circuit are labelled by the numbers in the circles in the graph. When you measure the output of a quantum circuit, you can get different states with different probabilities. For example, in tutorial level 1, the input state is 0. The output state has a probability of 0.5 of being measured in state 0, and 0.5 of being measured in state 1. This is represented by probability 'flowing' through the graph; 0.5 stays in the state 0, and 0.5 goes to the state 1. This probabilistic property of quantum circuits is because of the principle of superposition, where a quantum state can be in multiple basis states at the same time!

Higher levels show more complex circuits (the circuit underlying Levels 4 and 5 are shown on the right hand side), made up of multiple gates. We use a Hadamard (H) gate, that takes the 0 state to an equal superposition of 0 and 1, and a NOT gate (X), that takes 0 to 1 and vice versa. To find out more, check out https://qiskit.org/textbook/preface.html.

By moving the probability around a cycle, it is possible to change the edge values, but represent the circuit, as the total probability entering/leaving a node stays the same. The levels in Squamble show a mixed version of the circuit graph, where some edges have negative values, and the goal of the game is to rearrange the probability within the graph so that none of the edge values are negative.
